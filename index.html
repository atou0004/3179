Chocolate Visualisations

The Visualisations are based on ratings were compiled by Brady Brelinski, Founding Member of the Manhattan Chocolate Society on plain dark chocolate

Chocolate is one of the most craved food around the world. Women and Men are known to be pretty obsessed with it. People normally eats chocolate when they are feeling a bit hungry or wanting to satisfy their sweet tooth.

Nowadays there is quite a enourmous amount variety of modern and classic confections which are made of chocolate. Not only that, when you first heard of chocolate, the first thing that pop into your mind would be a picture of sweet tasting candy bar. Little did you know that it was actually made from a plant! The benefits of chocolate bring are due to cocoa content. This is one of the reasons why top producers from around the world care so much on using the finest and most flavorful cocoa beans when making chocolate candies that we have today. Additionally, top chocolate producers would hire professional taste testers to get important feedback, which can inspire them improve their product. That is why Manhattan Chocolate Society was formed.


Chocolate Bar's Average Rating Around The World

The choropleth map below shows the Dark Chocolate Bar Average Rating for each country for years (2016-2020). As you can see from the map, most of the countries that produces dark chocolate bar have similar average rating. With Chile having the highest average rating of 3.75, followed by Argentina, Poland and Sao Tome with an average rating of 3.50. Australia having the forth most highest average rating (3.37), right behind United Arab Emirates with an average rating of 3.38.

Furthermore, there is only one country (Ghana) in Africa that produces dark chocolate bar.


Chocolate Taste

The most important aspect of the Cacao Ratings is the flavour. Flavor diversity, balance, intensity, and purity are all taken into account. When considering flavour component, genetics, terroir, post-harvest techniques, processing, and storage can all be discussed.

Texture related issues can also have an impact on the flavour, as texture has a significant impact on the overall experience.

The Bar Chart on the left shows the top 5 most common tastes in a dark chocolate bar. It seems that nutty is the most common flavour in a dark chocolate bar.



Cocoa Percentage

Cocoa Percentage is the amount of chocolate producers chooses to put in their product. Dark chocolate usually has a cocoa percentage of 55% to 99%.

The error band chart on the right shows the percentage of cocoa used in dark chocolate bar from year 2016 to 2020. In 2014 dark chocolate bars tasted by Brady Brelinski has an average of 72.17% cocoa percentage.


Number of Ingredients

In the world of chocolate, there are many different recipes in making a delicious dark chocolate bar.

The Radical Plot on the left visualises the popularity of number of ingredients used to make a dark chocolate bar throught year 2016 to 2020. As we can observe from the visualisation, it shows that 3 number of ingredients is the popular number of ingredients to make up chocolate. 1 and 6 number of ingredients are the least propular number of ingredients to make up dark chocolate.


Cocoa Origin
